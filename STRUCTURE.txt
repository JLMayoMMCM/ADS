================================================================================
  EU ENERGY DASHBOARD — SECTION STRUCTURE AND CONTENT
================================================================================

This document describes the content, charts, filters, and interpretations for
each section of the EU Energy Analytics Dashboard (csv-visualizer).

Navigation is via the top bar. All sections share the same EU-themed layout:
  - Fixed navigation header (always visible, EU blue #003399)
  - Filter bar directly below the header (section-specific controls)
  - Main content area (fills the remaining viewport — no page scrolling)
  - Light / dark mode toggle (top-right of the navigation bar)


================================================================================
  SECTION 1: OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Purpose
  Landing view. Presents the EU-27 energy system at a high level — both
  electricity and heat — giving the user immediate context before exploring
  individual sections.

Filter bar
  Year Range Slider
    6 discrete stops: 1999, 2004, 2009, 2014, 2019, 2024.
    Adjusting this slider trims the charts to show only the selected time range.
    Selected years are highlighted in gold; the range is displayed to the right.

KPI Cards (4 cards, always show 2024 values)
  Total Electricity 2024    Total GEP for EU-27 in 2024 (GWh)
                            Trend indicator: % change versus 1999
  Total Heat 2024           Total GHP for EU-27 in 2024 (GWh)
                            Trend indicator: % change versus 1999
  Renewable Share 2024      Renewables & Biofuels as % of gross electricity
                            Gold accent; shows percentage-point gain since 1999
  Nuclear Share 2024        Nuclear as % of gross electricity in 2024
                            Shows 1999 baseline for comparison

Charts (2 charts, side by side, fill remaining height)

  Electricity vs Heat Production  [Line chart]
    Two lines on the same axis: EU-27 Gross Electricity Production (blue) and
    Gross Heat Production (gold), plotted in GWh per 5-year interval.
    Y-axis in millions of GWh (M GWh). Responds to year range slider.

    Insight: Electricity has remained broadly stable in the 2.5-2.9 TWh range
    while heat has declined approximately 4% since its 2004 peak as building
    efficiency standards improved across the EU.

  Electricity Source Mix  [100% Stacked Area chart]
    Three stacked bands showing the proportional share of Renewable, Nuclear,
    and Fossil sources in EU-27 electricity. Bands always sum to 100%.
    Renewable = green. Nuclear = dark blue. Fossil = slate.
    Responds to year range slider.

    Insight: Renewables surpassed Nuclear around 2009 and have grown
    continuously. Fossil share dropped from approximately 56% (1999) to
    approximately 33% (2024), confirming the structural shift underway.


================================================================================
  SECTION 2: ELECTRICITY
================================================================================

Purpose
  Deep-dive into Gross Electricity Production (GEP). Covers all fuel types
  across the full 1999-2024 time series with interactive filters.

Filter bar
  Year Range Slider      Same 6-stop slider; trims all charts to selected range.
  Fuel Chip Selector     Multi-select pill buttons for 10 fuel types:
                         Nuclear, Natural Gas, Solid Fossil Fuels, Lignite,
                         Oil & Petroleum, Hydro, Wind, Solar PV, Bioenergy, Waste.
                         "All" button to toggle all on or off.

KPI Cards (4 cards)
  Total GEP 2024         Total gross electricity in 2024 (GWh), % vs 1999
  Renewable Share        Renewables & Biofuels % of 2024 GEP; 1999 baseline
  Wind Share 2024        Wind as % of 2024 GEP; 1999 baseline
  Solar PV Share 2024    Solar PV as % of 2024 GEP (was ~0% in 1999)

Charts (4 charts in a 2x2 grid, fill remaining height)

  GEP by Fuel — Stacked Area  [Stacked area chart]
    Each fuel type is a stacked band showing absolute GWh output per period.
    Only fuels selected by the fuel chip filter are shown.
    Y-axis in millions of GWh.

    Insight: Renewables & Biofuels overtook Solid Fossil Fuels around 2009 and
    now represent the single largest category at 46.5% of total GEP.

  2024 Fuel Mix — Share of Gross Electricity  [Donut pie chart]
    Percentage breakdown of each fuel in the most recent year (2024).
    Label={false}: all values shown in the legend below as "Fuel: X.X%".
    Fuels with <0.05% share are omitted.
    Responds to fuel chip filter (removes deselected fuels from the pie).

  Nuclear vs Renewables  [Line chart]
    Four lines: Nuclear (dark blue), Renewables & Biofuels (green),
    Wind (sky blue dashed), Solar PV (gold dashed).
    Tracks the energy transition crossover point.
    Y-axis in thousands of GWh.

    Insight: Renewables crossed above Nuclear in 2014. By 2024 renewables
    produce nearly twice as much electricity as nuclear: 1,292 TWh vs 650 TWh.

  Fuel Mix % — Stacked Bar  [100% Stacked bar chart]
    Each bar represents one year and sums to 100% of gross electricity.
    Shows the structural shift in proportions year by year.
    Only selected fuels are included.

    Insight: Solid Fossil Fuels + Lignite fell from 41.8% (1999) to 15.7%
    (2024). Wind + Solar PV rose from 0.5% to 27.8% over the same period.


================================================================================
  SECTION 3: HEAT
================================================================================

Purpose
  Gross Heat Production (GHP) — district heat and combined heat & power (CHP)
  across the EU-27, broken down by fuel type.

Filter bar
  Year Range Slider      6-stop slider; trims all charts to selected range.
  Fuel Chip Selector     Multi-select for 8 heat fuel types:
                         Natural Gas, Solid Fossil Fuels, Lignite,
                         Oil & Petroleum, Renewables & Biofuels, Waste,
                         Nuclear, Solar Thermal.

KPI Cards (4 cards)
  Total GHP 2024         Total gross heat in 2024 (GWh), % vs 1999
  Renewable Heat 2024    Renewables & Biofuels as % of heat mix; 1999 baseline
  Gas Share 2024         Natural Gas as % of heat mix; labeled "largest single source"
  Fossil Share 2024      Combined Gas + Coal + Oil + Lignite as % of heat mix

Charts (3 charts: 2 in a top row, 1 below; proportional height 60% / 40%)

  GHP by Fuel — Stacked Area  [Stacked area chart]  (top left)
    Same structure as the electricity stacked area but for heat fuels.
    GWh per period per selected fuel. Y-axis in thousands of GWh.

    Insight: Total heat production peaked in 2004 (~708 TWh) and has since
    declined ~21% as building efficiency improved. Renewables & Biofuels nearly
    quadrupled (+293%) while Solid Fossil Fuels fell 63%.

  2024 Heat Mix by Fuel  [Donut pie chart]  (top right)
    Percentage breakdown of heat fuel mix in 2024.
    Same legend-with-values approach as the electricity pie.
    Fuels below 0.05% are hidden.

    Insight: Natural Gas remains the largest source at 32.2%, but Renewables
    & Biofuels grew from 8.5% (1999) to 34.9% (2024) — now the second
    largest and the main growth driver.

  Key Fuel Trends in Heat Production  [Line chart]  (bottom, full width)
    Four fixed lines: Natural Gas (blue), Solid Fossil Fuels (slate),
    Renewables & Biofuels (green), Waste (light slate, dashed).
    GWh per period. Responds to year range slider.

    Insight: Natural Gas declined ~5% from 1999 to 2024. Renewables & Biofuels
    quadrupled. Solid Fossil Fuels fell 63%. These three trajectories in one
    chart capture the entire narrative of EU heat decarbonization.


================================================================================
  SECTION 4: COUNTRIES
================================================================================

Purpose
  EU member state comparisons. Users can rank, sort, filter, and explore
  country-level electricity or heat production and renewable share.

Filter bar
  Elec / Heat toggle     Switches all charts between electricity and heat data
  Country search         Text input filtering countries by name
  Top N selector         Shows top 5, 10, 15, or all 27 countries in the bar chart
  Sort buttons           Sort by Total Production, Renewable %, or A-Z

Charts (3 charts: 2 in a top row, 1 below; proportional height 60% / 40%)

  Top N Countries — Horizontal Bar  [Horizontal bar chart]  (top left)
    Ranks the top N countries by total production (GWh) in 2024.
    Color coding: Gold = 1st place, lighter blue = 2nd and 3rd, dark blue = rest.
    Responds to Elec/Heat toggle, Top N selector, sort, and search.

    Insight (electricity): Germany, France, and Spain together account for
    approximately 48% of EU-27 total electricity production in 2024.

    Insight (heat): Germany, France, and Poland produce the most district
    heat in the EU, driven by established large-scale CHP networks.

  Renewable Share vs Total Production  [Scatter chart]  (top right)
    Each dot represents one EU country.
    X-axis: Total production (GWh). Y-axis: Renewable share (%).
    Hover tooltip shows country name, total GWh, and renewable %.
    Always shows electricity data regardless of Elec/Heat toggle.

    Insight: Countries in the top-left (small total, high renewable %) include
    Austria, Denmark, and Latvia. Large producers like Germany and Spain show
    mid-range renewable shares (~50-56%). The scatter reveals that scale and
    renewable share are largely independent.

  Renewable Share Ranking — All 27 Countries  [Vertical bar chart]  (bottom, full width)
    All 27 EU countries sorted by descending renewable share (electricity 2024).
    Color thresholds:
      Gold   = 70% or above (10 countries)
      Green  = 40% to 69%
      Blue   = 20% to 39%
      Slate  = below 20% (6 countries)
    X-axis: country labels at 45° angle.

    Insight: 10 EU countries exceed 70% renewable electricity share. Only 6
    fall below 20%. The EU-27 average is approximately 46.5%.


================================================================================
  SECTION 5: TRENDS
================================================================================

Purpose
  Long-run trajectory analysis comparing how fuel volumes and percentage shares
  have changed from 1999 to 2024.

Filter bar
  Year Range Slider      6-stop slider; trims all charts to selected range.
  Fuel Chip Selector     Multi-select for 6 trend fuels:
                         Renewables & Biofuels, Wind, Solar PV,
                         Nuclear, Natural Gas, Solid Fossil Fuels.

Charts (3 charts: 1 large top, 2 smaller bottom; proportional height 60% / 40%)

  Absolute GWh Output by Fuel  [Line chart]  (top, full width)
    Six lines showing raw GWh output per fuel per period. Only selected fuels
    are shown. Y-axis in thousands of GWh.
    Clear visual divergence: renewables rising, fossil fuels declining.

    Insight: Renewables & Biofuels grew from 378K GWh (1999) to 1,292K GWh
    (2024), an increase of +242%. Solid Fossil Fuels dropped from 744K to
    272K GWh (-63%). These two lines crossing is the visual signature of the
    EU energy transition.

  % Growth vs 1999 Baseline  [Line chart]  (bottom left)
    Percentage change for each selected fuel relative to its 1999 value.
    Chart is capped at +300% to -100% for readability. Wind and Solar PV
    values are far beyond +300% and appear at the chart ceiling.
    Y-axis domain: -100% to +300%. Zero reference line shown.

    Insight: Solar PV grew +345,276% from 1999 to 2024 (86 GWh to 297K GWh).
    Wind grew over +2,000%. These are not incremental improvements but
    technology substitution events. Nuclear declined 23.4%.

  2024 Change vs 1999 — Net Shift  [Bar chart]  (bottom right)
    A single bar per selected fuel showing the net percentage gain or loss
    from 1999 to 2024. Zero reference line shown.
    Green bars = net growth. Blue bars = net decline.
    Provides a snapshot summary of the entire 25-year transition.

    Insight: The color contrast (green renewables, blue fossil/nuclear) makes
    the structural shift immediately visible. No transitional ambiguity exists
    among the selected fuels — every fossil fuel declined; every renewable grew.


================================================================================
  SECTION 6: WEKA
================================================================================

Purpose
  Interactive explorer for machine learning model outputs produced in WEKA.
  Covers 6 models: 4 kMeans clustering models and 1 classification model
  (J48 + Random Forest).

  Each sub-view is selected via tab buttons in the filter bar. All sub-views
  include a plain-language interpretation panel explaining what the algorithm
  did, what each cluster or class means, and what the results imply for EU
  energy policy.

Filter bar
  Sub-view selector (6 tab buttons):
    Country Elec Clusters
    Country Heat Clusters
    Classification
    GEP Fuel Clusters
    GHP Fuel Clusters
    Fuel Mix Trajectory

  Content scrolls within the section if it exceeds the viewport.


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SUB-VIEW: Country Elec Clusters
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Algorithm: kMeans, k=3
  Dataset: 27 EU countries described by electricity production totals across
           6 periods (1999-2024) and 2024 fuel breakdown.
  WCSSE: see model output file Country_5yr_Elec_kMeans.txt

  Clusters:
    Cluster 0 — Med Renewable  (n=4, 15%)
      Countries with moderate output and mixed fossil/renewable portfolios.
    Cluster 1 — Low Renewable  (n=5, 19%)
      Countries dominated by fossil fuels or nuclear with limited renewables.
    Cluster 2 — High Renewable  (n=18, 67%)
      The majority of EU states; higher renewable proportions, often smaller
      producers where wind, solar, or hydro dominates the mix.

  Interpretation panel:
    Explains how kMeans partitioned 27 countries, what each cluster
    represents in terms of energy policy, and why the Large/High cluster
    is dominant (most EU countries are smaller producers with good renewable
    share). Low-Renewable countries are identified as primary policy targets.

  Charts:
    Cluster Distribution Pie
      Donut chart showing n and % for each cluster (3 segments).
      Cluster legend below with color, name, count, and percentage.

    Cluster Centroids — 5-Year Electricity Totals  [Grouped bar chart]
      4 bar groups (Full Data, Cluster 0, 1, 2) per year period.
      Shows the average electricity output per cluster per period.
      Reveals size gradient between large-producer and small-producer clusters.

    Cluster Centroids — 2024 Fuel Breakdown  [Grouped bar chart]
      4 bar groups per fuel category for 2024.
      Shows the average fuel profile (GWh per fuel) for each cluster.
      Highlights differences in fuel composition between clusters.


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SUB-VIEW: Country Heat Clusters
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Algorithm: kMeans, k=3
  Dataset: 27 EU countries described by heat production totals across 6
           periods and 2024 fuel breakdown.

  Clusters:
    Cluster 0 — Med Heat  (n=5, 19%)
      Moderate heat producers, averaging 44-55K GWh per period.
      Fossil-heavy mixes.
    Cluster 1 — High Heat  (n=3, 11%)
      Major district-heating economies averaging 70K+ GWh per period.
      Gas and Renewables dominant.
    Cluster 2 — Low Heat  (n=19, 70%)
      The large majority of EU countries with minimal district heat
      (<10K GWh per period). Primarily warm-climate economies.

  Interpretation panel:
    Explains the climate-driven asymmetry — why 19 of 27 countries are Low
    Heat producers — and the declining trend across all clusters from 2004
    to 2024. Identifies High Heat countries as the primary levers for
    EU district heating decarbonization given their scale.

  Charts:
    Cluster Distribution Pie
      Donut chart showing n and % for each cluster (3 segments).
      Cluster legend below with color, name, count, and percentage.

    Cluster Centroids — 5-Year Heat Totals  [Grouped bar chart]
      Average heat output per cluster per period. Shows the stark volume
      difference between the 3 High Heat countries and the 19 Low Heat.


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SUB-VIEW: Classification
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Task: Predict each country's RenewableShareClass in heat production.
  Target classes: Low / Med / High (renewable share of total heat)
  Features: 5-year heat totals and fuel breakdown per country
  Models: J48 Decision Tree and Random Forest (10-fold cross-validation)

  Results:
    J48 Decision Tree    64% accuracy (16/27 correct), Kappa 0.459
    Random Forest        56% accuracy (14/27 correct), Kappa 0.337

  Interpretation panel:
    Explains the classification task, why J48 outperforms Random Forest
    on a small dataset (n=27), what Cohen's Kappa means (moderate agreement
    beyond chance), which classes are easiest and hardest to predict, and
    the policy implication of identifying Low-renewable countries reliably.
    Notes that the "Med" class (borderline countries) causes the most
    misclassifications in both models.

  KPI Badges (4 metrics displayed prominently):
    J48 Accuracy (gold highlight)   64%
    J48 Kappa                       0.459
    Random Forest Accuracy          56%
    Random Forest Kappa             0.337

  Charts:
    Per-Class Metrics — J48 vs Random Forest  [Grouped bar chart]
      6 groups (J48-Low, J48-Med, J48-High, RF-Low, RF-Med, RF-High).
      4 bars per group: F-Measure, ROC Area, Precision, Recall.
      All values on 0-1 scale.
      Shows that "High" class has best metrics, "Med" is consistently worst.

    Confusion Matrices (2 side-by-side tables)
      Left: J48 confusion matrix (3x3).
      Right: Random Forest confusion matrix (3x3).
      Rows = True class. Columns = Predicted class.
      Diagonal cells highlighted green = correct predictions.
      Off-diagonal cells = misclassified countries.


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SUB-VIEW: GEP Fuel Clusters
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Algorithm: kMeans, k=2
  Dataset: 13 electricity fuel types described by renewable share ratio
           across 5 periods (1999-2019) plus 2024 absolute GWh output.
  WCSSE: 2.785 (converged in 3 iterations)

  Clusters:
    Cluster 0 — Low-Renewable Fuels  (n=10, 77%)
      Nuclear, Natural Gas, Solid Fossil Fuels, Lignite, Oil & Petroleum,
      Bioenergy, Waste, and others. Renewable share ratios below 21%.
      Average 2024 output: 213K GWh per fuel type.

    Cluster 1 — High-Renewable Fuels  (n=3, 23%)
      Hydro, Wind, Solar PV. Renewable share ratios of 77-83%.
      Average 2024 output: 738K GWh per fuel type.

  Interpretation panel:
    Explains what the binary clustering found, why Bioenergy falls in
    the Low-Renewable cluster (it co-combusts with fossil fuels in CHP),
    what the WCSSE indicates about cluster quality, and the significance
    of the 3× output gap between clusters. Notes the energy transition
    implication: only 3 fuel types drive the clean electricity surge.

  Model metric badges: WCSSE, iterations, fuel type count.

  Charts:
    Renewable Share Ratio — Centroid Trajectory  [Line chart]
      3 lines (Full Data, Low-Renewable centroid, High-Renewable centroid).
      X-axis: 5-year periods 1999-2019. Y-axis: 0-100% renewable ratio.
      Shows the consistent gap between the two clusters across all periods.

    2024 Production Total  [Grouped bar chart]
      One group ("2024 GWh") with 3 bars: Full Data, Low-Renewable, High-Renewable.
      Illustrates the disproportionate output from the High-Renewable cluster.


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SUB-VIEW: GHP Fuel Clusters
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Algorithm: kMeans, k=2 (mirrors GEP analysis for heat fuels)
  Dataset: Heat fuel types described by renewable share ratio and 2024 GWh.
  WCSSE: 2.298 (converged in 2 iterations)

  Clusters:
    Cluster 0 — High-Renewable Heat Fuels  (n=3, 23%)
      Solar Thermal, Geothermal, Bioenergy. Renewable share >= 69%.
      Centroid rose from 0.687 (1999) to 0.827 (2019).
      Average 2024 output: 153K GWh per fuel type.

    Cluster 1 — Low-Renewable Heat Fuels  (n=10, 77%)
      Natural Gas, Solid Fossil Fuels, Lignite, Oil, Waste, Nuclear.
      Centroid below 24% renewable share throughout.
      Average 2024 output: 41K GWh per fuel type.

  Interpretation panel:
    Explains why the heat clustering is even tighter than electricity
    (WCSSE 2.298 vs 2.785), notes Bioenergy's outsized 153K GWh centroid
    output for just 3 fuel types, and identifies the decarbonization
    lever: replacing Natural Gas with Bioenergy and Solar Thermal in
    district heating. Notes the even more binary nature of heat fuels
    — fossil vs. renewable — with almost no ambiguous middle ground.

  Model metric badges: WCSSE, iterations, fuel type count.

  Charts:
    Renewable Share Ratio — GHP Cluster Trajectory  [Line chart]
      3 lines showing High-Renewable, Low-Renewable, and Full Data centroids
      over 1999-2019. Same structure as GEP chart.

    2024 GHP Total — Cluster Centroids  [Grouped bar chart]
      Centroid GWh comparison: High-Renewable vs Low-Renewable vs Full Data.


  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  SUB-VIEW: Fuel Mix Trajectory
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Algorithm: kMeans, k=3
  Dataset: 12 electricity fuel types described by their % share of GEP
           across 6 time periods (1999-2024). Trajectory clustering —
           groups by the shape of the trajectory, not the absolute value.
  WCSSE: 3.036 (converged in 2 iterations)

  Clusters:
    Cluster 0 — Surging  (n=2, 17%)
      Solar PV and Wind. Near-zero shares in 1999; steep growth to 2024.
      Centroid: 0.26% share (1999) → 13.91% share (2024).

    Cluster 1 — Declining  (n=7, 58%)
      Nuclear, Natural Gas, Solid Fossil Fuels, Lignite, Hydro, Renewables
      aggregate, Oil. Historically dominant fuels losing or stagnating share.
      Centroid: ~18% across all periods (mixed trajectories average out).

    Cluster 2 — Fading Out  (n=3, 25%)
      Marginal fuels with small, declining shares across all years.
      Centroid: 6.68% share (1999) → 0.83% share (2024).

  Interpretation panel:
    Explains what trajectory clustering means (vs. value clustering),
    identifies which specific fuels belong to each cluster, explains
    why the Declining cluster has a stable centroid despite containing
    declining fuels (trajectories average out), and frames the three
    archetypes as a complete narrative of the EU energy transition:
    two fuels surging, seven declining, three disappearing.
    Notes this is the most analytically rich model in the project.

  Cluster size badges: n and % for Surging, Declining, and Fading Out.

  Charts:
    Centroid Share Trajectories  [Line chart]
      4 lines: Full Data (dashed), Surging, Declining, Fading Out.
      X-axis: years 1999-2024. Y-axis: % share of GEP.
      The Surging line rising steeply from near-zero is the most
      visually striking feature of the entire WEKA section.

    kMeans Centroid Values Over Time  [Grouped bar chart]
      3 bar groups per year (Surging, Declining, Fading Out).
      Raw centroid % values per cluster per year.
      Reinforces the trajectory shapes as a bar comparison.


================================================================================
  SHARED UI ELEMENTS
================================================================================

Navigation Bar (top, always visible)
  EU flag icon + "EU Energy Dashboard" title  (left)
  Section tabs: Overview, Electricity, Heat, Countries, Trends, WEKA  (center)
  Light/Dark mode toggle button with sun/moon icon  (right)
  Background: always EU blue (#003399) regardless of theme.

Filter Bar (below navigation, section-specific)
  Compact bar showing controls relevant to the current section.
  Year Range Slider: 6 stops, selected range highlighted in gold.
  Fuel Chips: color-coded pill buttons per fuel type.
  Country controls: text search + Top N selector.
  WEKA sub-view tabs: 6 buttons for model selection.

KPI / Stat Cards
  Compact cards displaying a single metric with label, value, and trend.
  Gold accent border for the primary renewable metric in each section.
  Up/down arrows with green/red color for trend direction.

Chart Cards
  All charts use the same card container: rounded border, card background,
  title, optional subtitle, chart area, optional insight text at the bottom.
  Insight text (labeled "Insight:") provides a one-sentence finding
  specific to that chart's data.

Tooltip style (all charts)
  Dark/light adaptive background with EU border color.
  Font size 12px, compact padding. Hover to reveal exact values.

Color palette
  EU Blue    #003399    Navigation, primary buttons, fossil fuel lines
  EU Gold    #FFCC00    Accents, 1st place, Surging cluster, Solar PV
  Green      #22C55E    Renewables, positive trends, correct predictions
  Mid Blue   #4B80D4    Natural Gas, secondary chart lines
  Slate      #64748B    Solid Fossil Fuels, reference lines, Full Data
  Dark Slate #334155    Lignite, Fading Out cluster
  Sky Blue   #38BDF8    Wind
  Light Slate #94A3B8   Waste, muted elements
  Red        #DC2626    Downward trends (light mode gold becomes red-ish)
  Orange     #F97316    Med cluster labels in WEKA views
